Murders in Moscow: The Bryan Kohberger Case

March 16, 2025

In this gripping episode of Crime Time Snacks, we delve into the harrowing case of the Idaho student murders that rocked the nation in November 2022. Four University of Idaho students - Kaylee Goncalves, Madison Mogen, Xana Kernodle, and Ethan Chapin - were brutally stabbed to death in their off-campus home.

In the early morning hours of November 13, 2022, an intruder entered a rental home near the University of Idaho campus in Moscow, Idaho. Inside, four students were sleeping: Kaylee Goncalves, 21; Madison Mogen, 21; Xana Kernodle, 20; and Ethan Chapin, 20. All four were stabbed multiple times with a large fixed-blade knife. Two other roommates in the house were unharmed.

The murders shocked the small college town and sparked fear throughout the community. For weeks, the case seemed to go cold, with no suspects named publicly and few details released by law enforcement. Students fled the campus, and residents of Moscow were on edge, many arming themselves or installing security systems.

Behind the scenes, investigators were piecing together evidence. Surveillance footage from the area showed a white Hyundai Elantra driving near the victims' residence around the time of the murders. This vehicle became a crucial lead.

On December 30, 2022, authorities arrested 28-year-old Bryan Kohberger, a PhD student in criminology at Washington State University, located just across the state line from Moscow. Kohberger was arrested at his parents' home in Pennsylvania and extradited to Idaho to face charges.

According to the probable cause affidavit, DNA evidence found on a knife sheath left at the scene matched Kohberger's DNA. Cell phone data placed him near the victims' home at least a dozen times before the murders, and his phone was turned off during the critical hours of the attack. Additionally, one of the surviving roommates reported seeing a figure dressed in black with a mask in the house that night.

Kohberger owned a white Hyundai Elantra matching the one seen on surveillance footage. Investigators also found that he had applied for an internship with the local police department and had studied criminal psychology and behavior.

The motive for the killings remains unclear. There is no known connection between Kohberger and any of the victims, though investigators continue to explore possible links.

Kohberger has been charged with four counts of first-degree murder and one count of felony burglary. If convicted, he could face the death penalty. His trial is expected to be one of the most closely watched criminal cases in recent years.

The case has raised questions about campus safety, the role of social media in criminal investigations, and the psychological profiles of those who study criminal behavior while allegedly engaging in it themselves.

In this episode, we explore the investigation that led to the arrest of Bryan Kohberger and the latest developments including the recently released 911 call audio.
